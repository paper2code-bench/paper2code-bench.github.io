Paper2Code: Measuring Language Models' Capacity to Implement Novel Machine Learning Research Ideas

Keunhong Park1, Utkarsh Sinha2, Jonathan T. Barron2, Sofien Bouaziz2, Dan B Goldman2, Steven M. Seitz1,2, Ricardo Martin-Brualla2
1University of Washington, 2Google Research
GitHub Leaderboard Submit Your Paper

Paper2Code is a benchmark for evaluating language models' ability to implement novel machine learning research ideas from academic papers.

Abstract

We present Paper2Code, a benchmark for evaluating language models' capacity to implement novel machine learning research ideas from academic papers.

Our benchmark consists of a diverse set of machine learning papers that introduce novel ideas, along with their corresponding implementations. We evaluate language models on their ability to understand the papers and generate correct implementations.

We show that current language models struggle with implementing novel research ideas, highlighting the gap between understanding research papers and practical implementation. Our benchmark provides a way to measure progress in this important capability.

Related Links

There are several related benchmarks and datasets for evaluating language models' capabilities:

The Stack is a large dataset of source code for training language models.

HumanEval evaluates language models on their ability to solve programming problems.

BIG-bench is a diverse benchmark for evaluating language models on a wide range of tasks.
